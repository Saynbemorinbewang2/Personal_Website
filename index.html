About

I am a dynamic, rigorous, and passionate individual, with a strong adaptability and a great thirst for learning. From my earliest years at university, I distinguished myself through my academic achievements and commitment, earning the esteem of my teachers.

Holding a bachelor's degree in computer science, I continued my studies with a master's degree in "Software Systems in Distributed Environments" at the Faculty of Science of the University of Ngaoundéré, where I graduated top of my class in 2024.

Since 2025, I have been a PhD student at INRIA , within the FUN team, under the supervision of Nathalie Mitton and Carol Habib. My thesis focuses on the design of smart IoT networks supporting sustainable manufacturing processes in Industry 4.0, a field at the intersection of embedded computing, distributed communications, and the digital transformation of industrial systems.

My interests include the Internet of Things (IoT), wireless sensor networks (WSN), edge computing, and embedded software. I am also passionate about video games, a stimulating universe that fuels my creativity, analytical mind, and sense of innovation.

And if you ever lose track of me, look near a tennis court or in a meeting room planning the next big project (or maybe doing both!).
